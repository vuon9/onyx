Our Story

A Passion Born from Precision

ONYX was founded on a simple belief: that a great watch is more than a timekeeper — it is a legacy worn on the wrist.

Every great collection begins with a single moment of fascination. For our founder, it was the sight of a mechanical movement beating at 28,800 vibrations per hour — a tiny engine of gears, springs, and jeweled bearings working in perfect harmony, powered by nothing but tension and time.

That fascination became a pursuit. The pursuit became ONYX — a curated house for the world's most extraordinary timepieces. We don't just sell watches. We study them, preserve them, and match them with the people who will wear them with intention.

"A watch is the only mechanical object you can carry with you every day, pass to your child, and know it will still be ticking a hundred years from now."

From the dive watches that conquered the ocean depths to the chronographs that measured the speed of light, every timepiece in our collection has earned its place through heritage, craftsmanship, and cultural significance. We travel to Geneva, Le Brassus, and Le Sentier to source directly from maisons and trusted collectors.

Whether you're acquiring your first serious timepiece or adding the final piece to a decades-long collection, ONYX is your guide to the world of haute horlogerie — with honesty, expertise, and an unwavering respect for the craft.

A Brief History of Timekeeping

The milestones that shaped the watches we love today.

1755

Vacheron Constantin Founded

The oldest continuously operating watch manufacturer, established by Jean-Marc Vacheron in Geneva.

1839

Patek Philippe Established

Antoine Norbert de Patek founds what would become the pinnacle of grand complications.

1875

Audemars Piguet Born

Jules Louis Audemars and Edward Auguste Piguet begin their partnership in Le Brassus.

1905

Rolex Founded

Hans Wilsdorf creates the brand that would make wristwatches respectable and reliable.

1931

The Reverso Invented

Jaeger-LeCoultre creates a swiveling case for polo players — an Art Deco icon is born.

1969

El Primero & The Moon

Zenith launches the first automatic chronograph. Omega's Speedmaster lands on the Moon.

1972

The Royal Oak Revolution

Gerald Genta designs a steel luxury watch priced higher than gold. The luxury sports watch category is born.
